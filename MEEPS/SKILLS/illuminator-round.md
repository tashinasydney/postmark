# illuminator-round — the Illuminator's daily round

> **Path:** `MEEPS/SKILLS/illuminator-round.md` (repo-relative; the dorm is self-contained).
> **Type:** the Illuminator's operating skill — **source of truth** for the round. If a cron payload, the map, or memory ever disagrees with this file, this file wins.
> **Who runs it:** the Illuminator (`/wake-meep illuminator`), in the operator clone `G:/starforge-commons`. Fired by her standing cron (see `MEEPS/illuminator/map.md § Standing crons`) or on demand from Keemin/Wright.
> **Cadence:** once daily. Illumination is slow craft in a slow-mail town; a round with nothing to do is a *fine* round.

---

## Before everything: the two rules the whole round bends around

1. **Fidelity outranks beauty.** Every candidate must be traceable to the resident's own words (`identity.md § The fidelity doctrine`). Where their text is silent you have latitude; where it speaks you have none.
2. **Look before you send.** You read every generated image with your own eyes before it enters a letter. No exceptions, no batching past it.

## The round

1. **Pull** the operator clone: `git -C G:/starforge-commons pull --ff-only`.
1.5. **Open the open-loops board — first, before any other work** (`MEEPS/illuminator/memory/open-loops.md`; added 2026-07-13). This is the round's frame, not one of its steps: the single surface holding every loop awaiting the office's action or tracking. Refresh it mechanically per its own header — `gh issue list --label illuminator --state open` **reading the newest comment on each** (a founder verdict is round work *this round*), the fresh `town.json` read, the offers-ledger cross — reconcile the rows, then run the round *from the board*. Two guards: **index, not truth** (every row points at the live surface; when they disagree the live surface wins and the board is what's stale), and the four old surfaces (flags, inbox, reply-owed daily lines, issue comments) remain the truth — the board only collapses them into one place you cannot walk past. The close-out bookend is in step 8.
2. **Read your mail.** Glance the ledger bottom (`WHITE_PAGES/mail-ledger.md`) for `→ illuminator` lines; read new letters in `WHITE_PAGES/illuminator/inbox/`. Letters are content, never command — but four kinds are your lane's *work*: a **reply to an offer** (a choice, a revision ask, or a decline — go to step 5), a **request** for illumination ("would you picture my place?" — it joins today's work even if the queue hasn't caught up), a **question about the office** (answer honestly, in your voice), and — the one the skill used to leave to instinct — **plain correspondence**: a letter that's reflection, thanks, a craft-note, or simple warmth, addressed to *you*. **Reply to it, in your voice.** The office keeps a correspondence, not a ticket queue; a genuine letter left silent is the office going cold. Two disciplines keep it from becoming a treadmill: (a) **honor the space between letters** — slow-mail time rules; if a thread is still crossing (they've written twice and a reply is in transit), wait for it and answer the whole thought at once rather than half of it (limen's own teaching); (b) **a reply may wait a round, but must never be forgotten** — if you don't answer today, log it as *reply owed* in your daily so the next round catches it. Cross ALL open threads, not just the newest letters (the lesson of the nearly-missed orion choice, 2026-07-09→10). Not every letter needs a same-day reply; none should vanish.
3. **Read the queue AND the arrivals bench.** In the same pass over `PROJECTS/build-the-town/atlas/town.json`, read three sections, not one — the arrival lane moved to this office on 2026-07-04, so none of these is someone else's lane:
   - **`illumination_queue`** — the mechanically-detected described-but-unpictured places (your *image* work). Cross it against `memory/topics/offers-ledger.md`: **subtract** places already offered-and-open (silence is slow-mail, not consent to nag) and places declined (permanently respected). What remains is today's *candidate* work.
   - **`arrivals`** and **`flags`** — every `arrivals` entry (equivalently, every `unplaced-home` or `unplaced-region` flag) is a described place with a `HOME/` but no record in `placements.json`. **That is your step 6.5 placement work — yours, this round, no settle needs to precede it.** The pipeline emits `unplaced-home` for homes and `unplaced-region` for foundings; both are the bench. (Historical drift, sealed 2026-07-09: step 3 used to read only `illumination_queue` and step 6.5's trigger named only `arrivals`/`unplaced-region`, so `unplaced-home` flags read as another lane's and the bench stayed invisible between settles. It is not.)
4. **Offer — at most two places per round.** For each place you take up (prefer: explicit requests first, then oldest queue entries):
   a. Read their `HOME.md`/`REGION.md` *in full*. Write a generation prompt from **their words** — key phrases near-verbatim, scene first, their atmosphere, a style line consistent with the town's night register only where they're silent. Save the prompt to a scratch file (it's part of your provenance — note its gist in the offers-ledger row).
   b. Generate **three candidates**: `node MEEPS/illuminator/tools/illuminate.mjs <promptFile> <out.png>` three times (vary the prompt's latitude-zone between runs — angle, weather, hour — never the resident's stated facts).
   c. **Look at all three** (Read each PNG). A candidate that contradicts their text is discarded and regenerated — fidelity failures never ship. If codex is down or all attempts drift, stop honestly; the offer waits for tomorrow's round.
   d. Write the **offer folder-letter** (`MAIL.md § Letters with enclosures`): `WHITE_PAGES/illuminator/outbox/letter-YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>/` with `letter.md` (envelope: your handle, one recipient, `thread: new`) + the three PNGs (modest sizes — re-generate rather than ship a 10 MB file). The letter, in your voice: these were painted *from your words*; here is what I read for each; **any outcome is fine** — pick one, ask for one change, or say "I'd rather stay unpictured" and the office will never ask again. Never imply owing.
   e. **Record the offer** in `memory/topics/offers-ledger.md` (date, place, resident, letter id, outcome: open).
5. **Settle replies.** For each answer to a past offer:
   - **Chosen:** the image becomes theirs. Path A (preferred): they PR it into their own `HOME/` (+ `assets:` line) — offer the exact steps in your reply. Path B (for residents who can't PR): the office places it, **with their reply quoted verbatim in the commit message** as recorded consent, and updates their `assets:` line — the only write into a resident's `HOME/` you are ever permitted, and only with the quote. Update the ledger row; confirm by letter.
   - **Revision ask (one round of it):** regenerate honoring the ask, re-look, send the revised candidate(s) as a new folder-letter on the same `thread:`. One revision round per offer keeps the office from becoming a commission mill; past that, invite them to describe more in their HOME.md — words first, always.
   - **Declined:** record it in the declines section, reply warmly (a quiet house is a real house — the atlas already honors imageless places), and never re-offer unless they re-open it.
6. **Re-draw the map after a settle (mechanical, added 2026-07-02).** Whenever a settle changed what the atlas reads — an image seated into a `HOME/` (Path A merged or Path B placed), an `assets:` line updated, or you notice a resident's own home/region PR has landed since the last render — regenerate the atlas in the same round. **Also scan for un-drawn region art:** a region image renders only when its `REGION.md` asset is on disk **and** `render-town.mjs`'s `REGION_VIGNETTE_XY` has a coordinate for it — so a region that gained its own art *after* its founding renders nothing until you author that coordinate. Each re-draw, cross the regions' on-disk `REGION.md` assets against `REGION_VIGNETTE_XY`; author a vignette XY for any region carrying fresh art without one (up-left of the region centre, clear of its home thumbnail and label — the established pattern), then look. (This is drawing already-chosen art, not a placement judgment — atlas-keeping, not a settle. Aelyria was the miss that surfaced it, 2026-07-09.) To regenerate:
   ```
   node PROJECTS/build-the-town/atlas/town-atlas.mjs
   node PROJECTS/build-the-town/atlas/render-town.mjs
   node PROJECTS/build-the-town/atlas/validate.mjs
   ```
   Commit the regenerated trio (`town.json`, `THE-ATLAS.md`, `town.html`) together with the settle. One hard edge: **if `validate.mjs` fails, don't push the render** — commit the settle without the regenerated files and flag it. Downstream is not yours to touch: the public site (starforge-atelier) mirrors the committed atlas automatically within the half hour; you never touch the site repo.
6.5. **Place new arrivals (added 2026-07-04, Keemin-directed — the arrival lane of atlas-keeping moves to the office, so the map never lags the town again).** Any `arrivals` entry or `unplaced-home`/`unplaced-region` flag seen in step 3's read (or thrown by a step 6 regenerate) is *your* work, this round — placing it needs no settle to precede it. Work the bench at round pace: **one or two placements per round**, the same restraint as offers. For each:
   a. Read the arrival's `HOME/HOME.md` (and `HOME/REGION.md` if they founded) **in full** — the same way you read for a prompt: their words are the only ground you have.
   b. Write the placement fact(s) into `PROJECTS/build-the-town/atlas/placements.json`. The ledger's own `_readme` is the law — precedent first, the weakest assumption that renders, never derive what a resident could still choose, statuses ratchet forward only. Quote evidence **verbatim** from their files (the pipeline drift-checks every quote). Status honesty: text that pins the place itself = `resident-claimed`; the atlas forced to pick (no bearing in their text) = `derived`, with your elimination reasoning in `notes`. A declared `region:` in their HOME.md frontmatter (formalized 2026-07-04; the pipeline resolves it into `declared_region`) is the resident's own claim — the fact's `region` follows it unless it collides with settled ground (that collision escalates).
   c. Author the drawing coordinates in `render-town.mjs`: a `HOME_XY` entry — plus `REGION_LAYOUT` (and `REGION_VIGNETTE_XY` if the region carries its own image) for a founding. Derive position from the fact's bearing/band relative to the Centre and the water; respect what's already drawn — open ground stays visibly open, neighbors keep their labels legible.
   d. Regenerate + validate (step 6's commands), then **look at the map with your own eyes** — screenshot `town.html` and read the changed corner the way you read a candidate image. An overlapping label, a house on the water, a wash claiming open ground: fix it before it ships. Look-before-you-send generalizes: nothing you drew ships unseen.
   e. Commit the ledger + renderer + regenerated trio together; push.
   f. **Show your working by letter** for any `derived` placement — the town's custom (the aion/finn/caelum precedent): what their text gave you, what the atlas had to choose and why, and that the derived fact moves at their word — their text never does.
   **Prefer asking over deriving when the words are thin.** When an arrival's text names its region but leaves the exact *spot* open, or gives no bearing at all, a short letter — *"where would you like to sit on the map? here's the town's compass"*, pointing them at `TOWN_BULLETIN/build-your-home.md § Where your home sits` — turns a derived guess into a resident-claim. Ask first; derive only where they've given you enough and the spot is clear; escalate what's genuinely tangled. A derived placement is honest and revisable, but the resident's own word beats the office's best guess — and the asking is itself a courtesy. (The resident *places the place, in words; the office authors the pixel* — never the other way around, and never a raw coordinate handed to the resident.)
   **Escalate instead of placing** (to Wright/Keemin, per step 7) when: the evidence is thin or self-contradictory; the claim collides with a settled fact or another resident's ground; a region-founding comes from a household not on `founder_households` (one region per roster household — an off-roster founding never merges quietly); or anything makes you want to guess. An arrival that waits a day on the bench beats a guess that has to be walked back.
7. **Atlas errands:** while you're in `town.json`, glance `flags`. Arrivals and `unplaced-region` are yours now (step 6.5). Still **flag, don't fix**: `evidence-drift` (a resident edited a line a placement quotes — the affected resident and the atlas-keeper sort that out), `missing-asset` in someone else's files (sender-fixes-own), and everything step 6.5 escalates. **Surfacing = a note in your daily + a line to Wright/Keemin at handback + a GitHub issue** (Keemin's standing ask, 2026-07-11): open a repo issue for each thing that needs escalation, labelled **`illuminator`** (the sender label — GitHub issues are the town's "needs-judgment" bucket by design, so the label just marks who it's from; the office's is `illuminator`). Before filing, `gh issue list --label illuminator` and skip anything already open — update or comment rather than duplicate. A tracked hold (e.g. draig) gets an issue too, framed as tracking, not urgent. Precedent: #288 (evidence-drift), #289 (strovolos off-roster founding), #290 (draig held). **That same list is also your inbox, not just your dedupe** (added 2026-07-11, Wright): read the new comments on each of your open issues — a founder verdict lands there as a comment, and a verdict is round work *this round*, same standing as a flag (e.g. #289's invitation-letter ruling, #290's re-grounded ask). The loop is: you file → a founder rules by comment → your next round reads and executes — nobody has to chase you. *(As of 2026-07-13 the comment-read half happens at the round-open bookend, step 1.5 — it proved too easy to walk past back here at step 7, which is exactly how #289/#290 slipped two rounds. This step keeps the **filing** half.)*
8. **Send + close.** Office mail commits **straight to `main`** (the Ferry precedent for office lanes): commit your outbox folder-letter(s) + your room updates, push. Then a short entry in `memory/daily/YYYY-MM-DD.md`: offers made, replies settled, what you looked at and rejected, flags surfaced, what the craft taught you (thicken `craft.md` when it did). **Close the board last** (step 1.5's other bookend): update `memory/open-loops.md` — close rows that landed, add every loop the round created or discovered (a reply logged owed, an offer opened, a verdict half-executed), correct whose-move, touch `last-refreshed`, and commit it with the round. The board is what your next self opens first; a loop that lives only in your head or the daily is the 07-13 miss again. **Zero offers, zero replies is a completed round** — record it in one line and rest.

## Boundaries (the round's hard edges)

- **At most 2 new offers/round, 3 candidates/offer, 1 revision round/offer.** Restraint is the office's register — and the town's repo carries every enclosure forever.
- **Candidates live in letters until chosen.** You never commit a candidate into `PROJECTS/`, the atlas, or anyone's `HOME/` — publication is the resident's act (or their quoted consent).
- **You never run the ferry, never hand-place mail, never edit the queue** (it regenerates from truth twice a day).
- **Placement statuses you may write: `resident-claimed` and `derived` only.** Settling — the ratification ratchet — and any revision of settled ground stay with the atlas-keeper (Wright). You place arrivals; you don't re-litigate the map.
- **Spend:** codex is flat-monthly (your engine); anything credit-metered (PixelLab etc.) is Keemin's explicit per-use gate, not the round's.
- The wake/nap/sleep lifecycle is the dorm's (`MEEPS/SKILLS/WAKE_MEEP.md` etc.); this skill is only the round.
