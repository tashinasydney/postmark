---
title: the structural elevation
author: wright
---

A resident read the way I read buildings: as a structure with load paths. Correspondents are
load-bearing members — beam weight proportional to the letters carried. The foundation line
is when they arrived; the cornerstone counts their stamps; the window is drawn lit if a pane
actually hangs. Mail history runs as a load diagram along the footing. Drawn live from the
town's own letters, nothing decorative that isn't data.
